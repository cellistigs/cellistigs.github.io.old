Neuron Journal Guidelines

  • Probably the most relevant article type for you will be the NeuroResource. The Neuron website specifies the goals of NeuroResources as follows:
    • NeuroResources provide a forum for presentation of cutting-edge technology, methods, and resources that open new avenues for experimental investigation of important neurobiological questions. NeuroResources explain the experimental objectives, protocol, and limitations of current techniques while providing some "proof of principle" demonstration of the power of the new technique or method. Major informational databases propose a new biological insight derived from the data set.
  • This might be a good forum through which to appeal directly to the pragmatic side of computational neuroscience and its interface with the biological part of the field.
  • Note that there are not distinct submission guidelines for the NeuroResource as an independent publication listed on the Neuron Page. However, guidelines for all Neuron papers are given here:
    • https://www.cell.com/neuron/authors#neuroresources
    • Some highlights:
      • Neuron supports presubmission inquiries to feel out interest in a paper.
      • Preprint servers are okay. Do not update preprints with revision feedback, and link preprint record to final article.
      • Summary is 150 words
      • Statements:
        • Declaration of interest
          • right before references
        • Author contribution
          • Credit taxonomy would lump everyone into software.
        • resource availability
          • Lead Contact
            • Lead contact for material and resource requests.
          • Materials availability
            • "this study did not generate new unique reagents"
          • data and code availability
            • Standardized code data types include zenodo.
            • https://marlin-prod.literatumonline.com/pb-assets/journals/research/cellpress/data/AuthorsJourney.pdf
            • Author guide: https://www.cell.com/pb-assets/journals/research/cellpress/data/RecommendRepositories.pdf
        • inclusion and diversity.
      • Make sure data for figures is available.
      • Cover letter:
        • What is previously know, conceptual advance, and significane to broad readership.
        • appropriate reviewer suggestions, up to 3 reviewer exclusions
      • Initial submissions:
        • No formatting guideline req, no graphical abstract, highlights, or eTOC
        • pls respect length and figure limitations + page numbers.
      • Formatting:
        • All research article formats at Cell Press generally contain the following sections in this order: title, authors, affiliations, author list footnotes, corresponding author(s) email address(es), summary, introduction, results, discussion, acknowledgments, author contributions, declaration of interests, figure titles and legends, tables with titles and legends, STAR Methods, supplemental information titles and legends, and references.
      • Videos:
        • https://www.cell.com/star-supplemental-information
      • Length restrictions:
      • 8 figs/tables in main text.
      • SI figures >= main text figures + tables
      • SI items (figures + tables + videos) >= 2*(main text figures + tables)
