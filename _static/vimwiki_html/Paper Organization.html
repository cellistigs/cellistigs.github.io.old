Paper organization is a way for you to structure your knowledge so that it's easier to search and more efficient to recall. What do you know about how your brain works w.r.t. papers?

  • It's way more fun to read papers as hard copies.
  • It's way easier to search and find papers online.
  • If you read a paper and find it interesting, you can recover the topic/gap with prompting in discussion up to a month later (about).
  • In general, you don't read enough literature before starting/deciding what to do next.
  • If you were to read 5 papers per week, you would be much happier with your understanding of knowledge, even if it's review.

End goal workflow:

  • Whenever you run into a new paper, upload it to your to read list in papers with the todo tag and appropriate list tags.
  • Ideally work through enough papers that this list is has approximate net length.
  • Upload the ref for new papers to anki, and link to the blog post that you've written/plan to write.
  • Whenever you read a paper, write a short summary, and then post the summary plus link to papers version to a github blog. Link to other related papers.
