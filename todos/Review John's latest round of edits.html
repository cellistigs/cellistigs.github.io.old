    • Write reproducibility debt paragraph for intro [time:15mins] #72a594b8
      • One consequence of IaGS is reproducibility debt. Reproducibility debt refers to the fact that it becomes more and more difficult to maintain the reproducibility of a scientific result as the infrastructure around scientific projects grows more complex (Sculley). Reproducibility debt is a consequence of countless undocumentented decisions that were made in the process of setting up and modifying scientific infrastructure, the high level of technical knowledge required to set up modern infrastructure, and the sheer number of components involved (all of the infrastructure described here, input data, and interactive workflow decisions that may only exist in our heads) (Hinsen). This can lead to analysis infrastructure that is neither fully described nor readily receated (citealp crook, stodden, krafczyk, raff) . Reproducibility debt incurred by the original analysis developer is compounded by individual analysis users when they install and manage their own software.
